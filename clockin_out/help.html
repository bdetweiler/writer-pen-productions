1. Introduction

1.1 What is Clockin/Out?

Clockin/Out is a PHP script with a MySQL backend that is used primarily for timestamp recording. Think of it as you would a regular time card. On both systems, times are recorded when you press the button. On both systems, times are shown in 24-hour time with 100 minutes per hour instead of the usual 60. Both systems have roughly an equal ammount, if not the same ammount of weaknesses and strengths. This FAQ will go over some of those.

For those wondering: the name Clockin/Out is a play on words. Think of it as "Clockin' Out" and "Clock In/Out".

1.2 On what systems does Clockin/Out run?

While Clockin/Out runs on its own server, you can use it from any machine with a web browser on the Aurora Cooperative network. The script itself runs on OpenBSD, a Unix-like BSD variant. The avarage user needn't be concearned with this though. The important thing is, if your machine has a web browser (Internet Explorer, Netscape Navigator, Mozilla, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, etc.) you can use it.

1.3 What licenses does Clockin/Out use?

Clockin/Out itself is produced under the GNU General Public License. The GNU General Public License (GPL) in a nutshell states that, should this script be distributed or sold in any way, the source code must be included. Why the GPL? There are many reasons for Open Source Software, but the primary one in this instance because of the backend database, MySQL. MySQL is dually-licensed, meaning it can be used with one or the other.

In order to use it freely under the GPL, the application using it must also be licensed under the GPL.

PHP is licensed under the PHP License, a less restrictive license which states that you may distribute your software with or without source code. It is compatible with the GPL. Apache is the worlds most popular web server which runs on a variety of platforms, including Windows, Linux, FreeBSD, and, in this case, OpenBSD. It is freely available under the Apache License. It is a GPL compatible license.

The operating system on which Clockin/Out runs is OpenBSD. It is made available under the BSD License, and can be found at http://www.openbsd.org.

1.4 Why switch from manual time cards to an automated system.

This question has been raised by a few individuals. The answer is "efficiency." Under the current system, it takes days to get all the time cards in, and then our employees have to figure the times manually using adding machines. This is a tedious job. One that can easily be remedied with the help of technology.

Though this may seem like an unnecessary change to some, from a CEO's standpoint, it can save time and money, and in the long run, help Aurora Cooperative to continue moving forward in Agricultural Business.

3. General Employees

3.1 Logging in

When logging in for the first time, your password will be identical to your username. Your username is your last name followed by your first innitial. Those with identical last names and identical first innitials have been taken into consideration. In this case, the person whose first name comes alphabetically last will use the first two letters of their first name.

Confused? Here's some examples:

Last Name First Name Username Password
Doe Joan doej doej
Doe John doejo doejo
When logging in for the first time, type in your username and password (both identitical), and press the "Submit" button. If you typed everything in correctly, you should see a page telling you that your password has expired. You are required to change it. Passwords must be six (6) characters in length, and may consist of alpha-numeric (numbers, letters, or both) characters and are CASE SENSITIVE. Keep this in mind when choosing a password.

When you have successfully changed your password, a screen will tell you so, and will redirect you to the login page. You must now login with your new password. This is done to ensure you have typed your password correctly. If you cannot get back in, doublecheck your typing. Make sure you are typing in the correct case. Is caps lock on? Should it be? If all else fails, a supervisor may reset your password to your username and you can try again.

3.2 Clocking in

To clock in, simply go to the login page, enter your username and password, select the "Clock In" radio button, check "Double Time" if necessary, and click the "Submit" button.

Once you press the "Submit" button, your time is recorded on the server. You will be taken to the main login page where any pertinant information will be displayed to you, and you can view your work hours, and do various other accounting from there.

3.3 Displaying your work hours

Once you are logged into the system, you may check your work hours. The display you see when logging in is your hours for the current pay period. If you don't see anything, then you haven't logged any hours for the pay period yet.

If you are clocked in and don't see anything, you must wait until you clock out to see your entry. This is different from the manual time cards in that on a manual card, you can see what time you clocked in at immediately. The reason this is different is because Clockin/Out computes your hours based on your clock in time and clock out time. With no clock out time, it has nothing to compute.

3.4 Logging time off

To log time off, such as vacations, personal holidays, sick days, or funerals, you will need to log in. If you are already clocked in, you may just click the "Neither" button to login without doing anything.

Once you are in, click on the tab. This will bring you to the page for logging days off. To take a single day off, you only need to enter the date into the "Start Date" field, select the type of hours, and select how many hours (4 hours = half day; 8 hours = full day). Then press the "Submit" button.

Dates must be entered in this format: YYYY-MM-DD

     Example: 2004-09-07, would be September 7th, 2004.

To take multiple days off, you need to enter an "End Date" in addition to a start date. This means you are taking a sequence of days off. For example, if you wanted to take 3 days off in a row, you would type the first date in the "Start Date" field, and the last day in the "End Date" field. You may also enter an end date for a single day. For a single day, you would enter the same date in both fields, however this is redundant and unnecessary.

You may log days off for the past, present, or future.

3.5 Making a note

There is a feature in Clockin/Out that allows you to make a note on an existing entry. Why would you want to do this? There are a host of reasons. Sometimes you just need to keep your boss updated on things. Sometimes you need to make a specific comment about an entry, like where you were. If you worked at a different location, you could put that in there. You can do the same thing on a time card. Remember, Clockin/Out was designed to mimmick the time card system. The goal is to incorporate all of the same features of a standard time card system.

To make a note, click the tab. Select the entry you wish to annotate from the drop down box. Write your note in the text field, and press the "Submit" button. You must keep entries to 180 characters, due to database restrictions. This should be more than enough to leave a descriptive note about your entry.

3.6 Changing your password

If you wish to change your password, you may do so by going to the tab. As stated earlier, the first time you log in, this is mandatory. After that, you may chose whatever password you wish. If you do choose the same password as your name, you will be forced to change it the next time you login. Clockin/Out does not allow you to have identical username/password combinations. This is obviously for security.

3.7 Logging out

When you are finished with your session, you may either close the browser window, or click the tab. When you click the logout tab, you will be redirected to the login page.

3.8 Clocking out

Clocking out is virtually the same procedure as clocking in, except you select the "Clock Out" radio button instead of the "Clock In" one. Once you have entered your correct username and password and selected "Clock Out", you may press the "Submit" button. The instant you press it, you will be clocked out.

You are then taken to the main work hours screen and told the time when you were clocked out.

4. Administrators/Supervisors

Throughout this text, and in Clockin/Out, the terms "Administrator" and "Supervisor" are used interchangeably. If you are a Supervisor, you are an administrator, and vise-versa. If you are a Supervisor, you are responsible for your employees. You must make sure they clock in and out on time, and that they are not abusing the system.

You are also responsible for fixing any mistakes in their entries. These steps will be discussed below.

4.1 Logging in

The admin side of Clockin/Out is different from the employee side. It uses two seperate accounts. Though your username will be the same, you may opt to have a different password. It is recommended that you do so, but is not required. The first time you login to the admin side, your password is your username. If your name is John Doe, your username is "doej" and your password is "doej" the first time you log in. (See the general employee login procedure).

Once you have logged in, you will be required to change your password. It can be a combination of letters and numbers, and must be at least 6 characters long. Why so long? Most websites, such as MSN Hotmail, Yahoo!, and others require at least 6 characters for your password. Most online banking requires 8 characters. 6 is pretty standard.

After you change your password, you will be logged out and redirected to the employee login page. Click on the "Administrators click HERE" link. Login as before with your new password. You are now free to go about your daily business.

4.2 Changing your password

Anytime you like, you may change your password. Click on the tab to do so. You will be logged out and redirected to the employee login page. Click on the "Administrators click HERE" link. Login as before with your new password.

4.3 Displaying users' work hours

When you login as an administrator, assuming you have already logged in once and changed your password, the first thing you will see is the "Work Hours" screen. Here you may view employees' work hours. To do so, select the employee you wish to view from the drop down list. Employees are displayed by username, which is their last name and first innitial. You can jump to their name faster by clicking on a name in the list and typing the first letter of their last name. It will jump to the first person with that letter in their last name.

After you have selected them from the drop down box, you may press "Go" and it will display their work hours for last pay period. If you wish to view their work hours for this pay period, or for any other duration of time, select it from the drop down list above the employee's name. All options are self-explanatory. To display the work hours for the entire month, select "Entire Month". Do the same for 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, 180 days, year to date, or previous year.

4.4 Displaying reports

This option is available to privilaged users (if you don't see the option, you don't have privilages). The purpose is to show a summary of all users' total work hours for the last pay period, and the one before that.

If you have this option, you will see the following on the work hours page:

Reports:
NEW OLD
Create Create
Download* Download*
  Archive


The new report will display a complete employee listing of total work hours for the latest pay cycle. The old option will show the pay period before that. Each monday morning paycycle, the server will automatically compile the reports for downloading. The only reason to create a new report would be if you made a change to an older date after the monday of a new paycycle. Then, the reports would not be fresh so you would have to create a new one. Keep in mind, this is taxing on the server, and can take about five to six minutes. This can also hold up other users from clocking in and out. Try to do this later in the day, if absolutely necessary.

The archive section holds two years worth of pay cycle reports. The reports are never removed from the server, but only two years worth will be displayed. Their file names reflect their pay cycle dates.

4.5 Auditing an account

This is one of the most important features of the administrator side. You, as an administrator/supervisor, will need to be able to audit your employees' accounts for various reasons. For example: An employee forgets to clock in one day. It is your job to go in and add an entry for him or her. What if an employee forgets to clock out? You will need to correct that entry for them. The process is explained in detail below:

Selecting users:
If you have selected a user to display their work hours, then you need only to click on the tab, and that employee's work hours will be brought up for auditing. If you have not selected an employee, you will be asked to select one first. You may switch to a new employee at any time.

Deleting entries:
Why would one want to delete an entry? If someone has the power to add an entry, they sure better have the power to delete it. You may select multiple entries at once. Hold down the ctrl key while clicking, or to select multiple entries in a row, click on the first entry, hold the shift key, and click on the last entry. Those entries and all the entries in between will be selected. Entries marked with three astrisks (***) are flagged with errors. This is meant to be more helpful for the administrators who will need to quickly find these entries.

Correcting an entry:
Here is the scenario: Bob clocks in late. Bob clocks out on time. Bob wants to be paid for what he actually worked. You are able to fix this. First, you select Bob's entry from the drop down box. Then, because you want to correct his clock in time, you enter the correct clock in time in the first box. This is given in the following format: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS

example: 2004-09-07 08:00:00 would be September 7th, 2004, 8:00 am.

Then, because his clock out time is correct, you leave the box below it as-is. Press "Correct" and, assuming you typed everything in right, the entry will have been corrected.

Here is another scenario: Bob clocks in on time. Bob clocks forgets to clock out. Bob sees a big error flag warning him that he has been clocked in for more than 18 hours. To fix this, you would select his entry from the drop down box. Bob has an error flag, so the entry will be marked with three astrisks (***) to make it easier to find. Because his clock in time is correct, you leave that as-is. You would then enter the correct clock out time in the box below it. Once you have entered that, press "Correct" and you're set.

One final scenario: Bob is having a bad day. He forgot to clock in, so he clocked in as soon as he remembered. Bob also forgot to clock out and was left clocked in all night. How do we fix this? This is roughly a combination of the two previous scenarios. Select the offending entry from the drop down list. Enter the correct clock in time in the top box. Enter the correct clock out time in the box below it. Select "Remove Error Flag" to get rid of his error flag, and press "Correct". Now you're on your way to making Bob's day better.

If at any time you wish to remove an error flag, you may do so by selecting the entry (obviously you would want to select an entry with astrisks (***), because these are the flagged ones) from the drop down box, checking the "Remove Error Flag" checkbox, and pressing "Correct".

You may also alter their hours from "regular time" to "double time" and vise-versa, should the need arise.

Adding an entry:
Adding an entry is different from correcting one, in that you are actually adding an entry from scratch, rather than manipulating an existing one. In this field, you will need to enter both a clock in time, and a clock out time. For an example of the time/date format, see Correcting an entry above. When you press "Add", your entry will be added into the database. Should this not be to your liking, you need only to delete it with the Delete entry option. See Deleting an entry for details.

Entering paid time off:
This option was originally included to make up for the fact that employees were not allowed to log time off for days in the past, including the present day. This has been changed to allow employees to enter days in the past, present, and future; however, the administrator should have control over this as well. For more information on how to use this tool, see Logging time off on the employee side.

4.6 Entering a commission

Some employees work on commission. To enter a commission for an employee, simply click on the tab. Once there, select the employee who will be receiving the commission. Then select the date. The dates are the beginning of each pay cycle. You can only enter one commission for each pay cycle, so if you need to enter more than one, you will have to add them up before entering them. Next, enter the ammount of the commission in dollars in this format: $$$$.cc, where $$$$ is Dollars, and cc is cents. Example: 1499.98 is One thousand four hundred ninety-nine dollars and ninety-eight cents. When you are satisfied with your entry, press "Submit" and your commission will be entered.

4.7 Resetting a user's password

If an employee/supervisor forgets their password, you have the ability to reset it to their username. Just click on the tab. Select the name of the employee you wish to reset. Now choose whether to reset only the employee side of the account, only the supervisor side of the account, or both. Then press the "Submit" button. Their password has been reset to their username, and they must login and change it like they did the first time they logged in.

4.8 Adding and removing users

This option is only available to privilaged users. The reason for this is that if a user is removed, they are completely removed from the system. Work hours and all. Therefore this is not something to be taken lightly. You should make absolutely certain that you do not need this employee in the system anymore. To remove a user, select them from the drop down list, double and triple check that you have the right employee, and press "Remove User".

To add a user, you simply need to enter their username, their first name, and their last name, check the checkbox if you wish them to be a supervisor, and press "Add User". If you forgot to add them as a supervisor, you may go back and enter them in again, this time checking the check box. You will be given an error that this user already exists, then it will confirm that they have been entered into the admin database. This indicates a successful entry.

4.9 Viewing a user's commissions

To view a user's commissions, you need to be in the work hours menu. Click on the tab. Select them from the drop down list. Select how you want to display hours, if necessary. Click "Go". Click the at the bottom of the work hours, and it will pop up another menu. Select the pay cycle for which you want to view the commissions. It will then tell you if there is a commission for this user on the pay cycle you selected.

When finished, the reports page will include all commissions for the pay cycle. The reports page is only for privilaged users.

4.10 Seeing who is currently clocked in

A useful tool to help diagnose problems, as well as give you the ability to determine which of your employees are and are not currently clocked in, is the "WHO" page. You will need to be at the "Work Hours" page. Click on the tab. At the bottom of the screen, you will see a link that says "See WHO is currently clocked in." Click on the "WHO" link, and a window will pop up telling you who is currently clocked in.

It is up to you to determine the use of this feature. If you don't know what to use it for, you don't need it.

4.11 Logging out

To logout of the system, simply click on the tab, or close your browser window. When you click the logout tab, you will be redirected to the employee login page.

5. Miscellaneous

This is where miscellaneous frequently asqued questions will be answered.

5.1 How does Clockin/Out keep track of my time?

Clockin/Out is web-based implementation of a time card system. When you press the "Submit" button on the login page and clock in, the server records that exact time into a database. It uses time based off of server it runs on. You can not control that time.

5.2 What if the server or Clockin/Out crashes?

Should the application crash, the server only needs to be reset. If the server crashes, rebooting does not work, and all the information is lost, the database is backed up nightly and taken off-site. It will be possible to restore the old information. Should that fail, work hours will have been printed out on a regular basis so as to have hard copies on file.

During any down-time, times can be recorded manually on the old time cards, and entered later, via the audit acount feature.

5.3 What if my internet connection is down?

As stated above, you would simply record your times manually on the old time cards and enter them later. No big deal.

5.4 What if I forget to clock in or out?

Your time can be entered manually by your supervisor. If your supervisor has questions about this, they should read the Administrator/Supervisor section, particularly the section on auditing an account.

5.5 Can I clock in or out from home?

No. You must be on the Aurora Cooperative network, either locally, or through a VPN connection.

5.6 Can another employee clock me in or out?

Of course. If you give them your password. This is the equivalent to asking an employee to punch your card after you've left for an hour. As stated above, this system contains almost all of the same pros/cons as the manual system.

5.7 Can another employee see my hours?

It depends. The answer is yes, if they have your password, or if you left your account logged in and walked away. The answer is also yes if they are a supervisor. Otherwise, no.

5.8 Should I let Internet Explorer/Netscape/Mozilla remember my password?

If you do, others will be able to log into your account if they have access to your computer (see above). It can be a convenience, or a security vulnerability. If you are a supervisor, the answer is no. Regular employees should use their better judgement with this one.

5.9 - My web browser automatically puts my password in for me, and I don't want it to. How do I disable this and remove the stored password?

In Internet Explorer, click on Tools, then click on "Internet Options". Click on the "Content" tab, and click the AutoComplete button. Uncheck the AutoComplete boxes so it won't ask again, and click the "Clear Passwords" button.

In Netscape Navigator 7.1, click on Edit, then click on "Preferences". Click on the "Privacy and Security" menu. Click on the "Passwords" option. Click the "Manage Stored Passwords" button. Remove the password you want to remove. Uncheck the "Remember Passwords" box.
